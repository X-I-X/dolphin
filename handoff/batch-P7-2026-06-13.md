## Content notes

### Page descriptions

**nyTimes.html** — NYT obituary by Andrew C. Revkin, published October 7, 2001, one week after Lilly's death. Original article was scanned from print; OCR artifacts preserved verbatim (e.g., "tnto" for "into", "tn wh;ch" for "in which"). Full text verified against original johnclilly.com source.

**washingtonPost.html** — Associated Press obituary published in Washington Post on October 4, 2001, four days after Lilly's death. Shorter piece focused on the isolation tank and interspecies communication work. Full text complete.

**wsjletter.html** — Lilly's response letter to the Wall Street Journal (1997) rebutting their "Save the Whalers" op-ed. Lilly's argument: cetacean brain sizes surpass human brains in associational cortex development; the body-size excuse for large brains ignores rotational stress physics research from WWII. Important historical document on Lilly's cetacean rights advocacy. Full text complete.

**koontz01.html** — Brief page with a letter from thriller author Dean Koontz to Lilly, accompanied by the cover of Koontz's book. Content is minimal (1–2 sentences) — this was a simple fan letter/tribute page on johnclilly.com.

**loehrx.html** — 1996 Franklin Loehr Memorial Award page. Text is minimal: award title + brief presentation note. One image (plaque.gif) preserved as slider. Franklin Loehr was an early researcher in parapsychology and New Science circles who knew Lilly.

**sayonara.html** — Misnamed in context: "SAYONARA" refers not to a farewell/memorial page but to the Shockwave audio demo for the "cogitate" repetition experiment (from *The Mind of the Dolphin*). When the word "cogitate" is repeated on a loop, the brain begins to hear 2,730 alternates over ~300 expert observers. The .dcr file was the audio file; the text explanation is the content of the page. Content verified complete.

**ashes00.html** — Photos from the spreading of John C. Lilly's ashes on the Pacific Ocean off Maui, January 6, 2002 (his birthday), photographed by Karl Straub. Three photos preserved as sliders. External link to karlstraub.com preserved.
