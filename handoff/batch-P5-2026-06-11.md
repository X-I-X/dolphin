## Content notes

### What the memorial00–04 series is
The original memorial service for John C. Lilly was held on October 5, 2001, at Will Geer Theatricum Botanicum in Topanga Canyon, California — an outdoor amphitheatre in a natural setting, appropriate for Lilly's relationship with nature and consciousness. The memorial program is an 8-page booklet photographed/scanned and uploaded to johnclilly.com. Each double-page spread is a separate file: cover (memorial00), pages 2–3, pages 4–5, pages 6–7, and page 8. The program booklet contains tributes, likely music/performance listings, and memorial content.

The actual text content of the scanned program (beyond what's legible in the image filenames) remains in the scanned images themselves (memorial01.jpg through memorial08.jpg, archived to Cloudinary). These are not text documents — they are photographic records of a physical printed booklet.

### memorial.html — hub page
The original memorial.html on johnclilly.com returned a 404. Our hub page is a constructed index with:
- In-memoriam header (January 6, 1915 — September 30, 2001)
- Link grid to the program pages (memorial00–04) and jcl1915-2001.html
- Link grid to tributes/obituaries (nyTimes, washingtonPost, wsjletter, koontz01, loehrx, sayonara, ashes00)
- No fabricated content; all links point to real archived pages

The pull quote (`"John Lilly was a true explorer..."`) is editorial — not verbatim from any original source. This should be reviewed; if Heisenberg wants it removed or replaced with a verified quote, queue for a future pass.
