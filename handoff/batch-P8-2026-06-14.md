## Content notes

### About this chapter

*The Mind of the Dolphin* (1967) is Lilly's most sustained scientific argument for cetacean intelligence and interspecies communication. Chapter 7, "Consideration of the Spiritual Side," is a philosophical departure from the laboratory research — Lilly argues that purely pragmatic science misses the inner motivation behind inquiry, and that awe, reverence, and spiritual openness are legitimate scientific tools, not embarrassments. He bridges this to cetacean research: if human minds exceed our purely rational model, the dolphin mind (larger by body-ratio measures) may exceed ours.

The chapter unfolds across 16 sub-pages of roughly 300–400 words each. All text verified verbatim against johnclilly.com; no truncation found on any page.

### OCR artifacts preserved verbatim (per content-fidelity rule)
The original johnclilly.com scan contains OCR errors throughout (e.g., "tbe" for "the", "industrv" for "industry", "hcld" for "held", "iife" for "life", "mto" for "into"). These are preserved verbatim as Lilly's original text — not corrected.
