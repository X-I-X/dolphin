## Content notes

### What this series is
*Programming and Metaprogramming in the Human Biocomputer* was written 1967–68 as a final report to the National Institute of Mental Health summarizing five years of Lilly's LSD/isolation tank research. It was initially too controversial to circulate widely; after Lilly left NIMH and went to Esalen, Stewart Brand (Whole Earth Catalog) helped distribute it. Julian Press published the first commercial edition in 1972. The work introduced the "biocomputer" metaphor for the human mind and the concept of metaprogramming — that a conscious agent can reprogram its own programs.

The 7 sub-pages (metaprog01–07) are individual chapters/sections. metaprog04 (Author's Note) and metaprog05 (Preface to Second Edition) are the same texts present on the main metaprog.html page — the original site had them duplicated. This is correct and expected.
