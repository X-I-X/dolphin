## Content notes

### dyadic15 — Chapter 15: The Dolphins Revisited
Lilly and his wife Toni outline their plan to return to dolphin research after closing the Communications Research Institute in 1968. Chapter covers: dolphin brain anatomy (Morgane/Jacobs/Yakovlev studies, 11,000 microscopic sections), the 3-emitter sonic system (nasal + laryngeal), sonar discrimination capability (aluminum vs copper disc at 100% accuracy), dolphin ethics/mutual dependence (voluntary respiration — they must wake unconscious fellows), oceanaria's role in public education, and their new computer-based communication program. OCR artifacts from the original scan preserved verbatim (e.g., "wery" for "very").

### dyadic16 — Chapter 16: Searching for Evidence of Extraterrestrial Beings  
Lilly describes the secret 1961 Green Bank Conference (National Academy of Sciences, sponsored by Frank Drake's Project Ozma), where he presented dolphin evidence alongside radio astronomers including Bernard Oliver. Core argument: two intelligent species already exist on Earth; dolphin communication research offers a more practical and immediate return than radio telescope arrays. Covers: Herman Wouk persuading Lilly to write popular books, the publishing story of *Man and Dolphin*, Project Cyclops, and Lilly's later suggestions for non-radio communication methods. Chapter links to Carl Sagan's *Intelligent Life in the Universe* (with Shklovsky) and *The Cosmic Connection*.
