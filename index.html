Cetacean Nation

The Intelligence of the Sea

Lilly was the first scientist to systematically argue that cetaceans — dolphins, porpoises, and whales — possess intelligence comparable to or exceeding that of humans. Half a century of research into the minds of the ocean's other great intelligences.

The Margaret Howe Experiment

A Woman Lived with a Dolphin for Ten Weeks

1965. Margaret Howe moved into a flooded house in the Virgin Islands with a young dolphin named Peter. Her diary records, week by week, the emergence of something that looked — from certain angles — like language.

Isolation Tanks

He Invented Solitude

In 1954, Lilly designed the first sensory deprivation tank at the National Institute of Mental Health. What began as a neuroscience experiment became a technology of inner exploration used by millions worldwide.

Brain & Mind

The Human Biocomputer

Long before Silicon Valley, Lilly proposed that the brain is a programmable biological computer — and that human consciousness is software running on it. His metaprogramming theory anticipated decades of cognitive science.

Metaprogramming · Simulations of God · NMR scans · Cognitive science

E.C.C.O.

Earth Coincidence Control Office

After years of deep isolation tank sessions and psychedelic exploration, Lilly developed a cosmological framework he called E.C.C.O. — the Earth Coincidence Control Office. A theory about the organizing intelligence behind meaningful coincidences.

Biography

A Life Without a Box

Born in St. Paul, Minnesota in 1915. Caltech. Penn. NIMH. The Virgin Islands. Esalen. Malibu. Lilly spent his life leaving institutions as soon as they wanted to contain him. He died in 2001 leaving behind a body of work that still has no category.
